üî• Structure Fire Response

1. Incident Command (IC) Setup
Assign a single IC upon arrival (NIMS). Report using LCAN (Location, Conditions, Actions, Needs). Assign Safety, Operations, Planning, Logistics sections. SOPs: 900.03, 900.44.
2. 360¬∞ Size-Up + SLICERS
Conduct full walk-around. Note fire extension and collapse hazards. SLICERS: Size-Up, Locate fire, Isolate utilities, Cool from safe positions, Extinguish, Rescue, Salvage. Essentials Ch.17; SOP 900.44.
3. Structural Considerations
Lightweight truss floors collapse within 5‚Äì10 mins. Watch for signs: bowing floors, heavy hose stream. Company Officer Ch.9.
4. Offensive vs Defensive
Offensive: interior attack when conditions allow. Defensive: exterior master streams when collapse risk or fire out of control. Announce mode on radio. Essentials Ch.16; SOP 900.44.
5. Rapid Intervention Team (RIT)
RIT placed immediately after primary search. Carry spare SCBA, forcible entry tools. PAR accountability every 10 minutes. SOP 900.25.
6. Ventilation Tactics
Vertical vent over fire room when safe; horizontal vent to release heat and smoke. Coordinate with interior attack. Essentials Ch.18.

‚ò£Ô∏è Hazardous Materials Response

1. Substance ID & ERG
Use placards to identify UN#, consult ERG Orange book. Refer to Green pages for protective actions. ERG 2024; SOP 900.23.
2. Establish Zones
Hot Zone (exclusion), Warm Zone (decon), Cold Zone (staging). Mark with tape and signs. SOP 900.23; 1000.01.
3. DECIDE Model
Detect hazard; Estimate risk; Choose responses; Identify options; Do response; Evaluate outcome. Company Officer - HazMat chapter.
4. PPE Levels
Level A for unknown high risk; B for splash; C for airborne; D for minimal risk. Fit-test SCBA, inspect suits. SOP 400.12.
5. Decontamination
Use triple rinse shower in warm zone. Collect runoff for disposal. Use soap/water; brush contaminants off. SOP 1000.02.

Stroke

FAST & Pre-Alert
Face droop, Arm drift, Speech slur, Time. Record onset time, pre-alert stroke center. SOP 1300.4.

Overdose

IV/IM 0.4‚Äì2mg, titrate to breathing. Support ventilations with BVM until spontaneous. SOP 1300.5.

Pediatric Care

Pediatric Assessment Triangle
Assess Appearance, Work of Breathing, Circulation to skin. Use Broselow tape for dosing. Essentials Ch.12; SOP 1300.9.

MCI Triage

START Triage
RPM: Respirations, Perfusion (radial pulse), Mental status. Tag: Red (immediate), Yellow (delayed), Green (walking wounded), Black (deceased). SOP 900.21.
