üî• Structure Fire Response

1. Incident Command (IC) Setup
Assign a single IC upon arrival (NIMS). Report using LCAN: - Location, Conditions, Actions, Needs Roles: Command, Safety, Operations, Planning Reference: Company Officer Ch.4; HFD SOP 900.44
2. 360¬∞ Size-Up + SLICERS
Walk around structure: note fire spread, collapse risk. Use SLICERS: Size-Up, Locate, Isolate, Cool, Extinguish, Rescue, Salvage. Reference: Essentials Ch.17
3. Offensive vs. Defensive Strategy
Offensive = interior attack when rescue or quick control possible. Defensive = exterior when collapse risk or heavy fire. Announce strategy and assign roles. Reference: Essentials Ch.16; Company Officer Ch.9
4. Tactical Benchmarks
Progress reports every 10 min: - Primary Search Complete - Fire Knockdown - PAR Complete Request additional alarms based on spread and resources. Reference: HFD SOP 900.44
5. RIT & PAR
Assign RIT with full PPE, spare air, tools. Stage at entry; perform PAR every 10 min or after mayday. Reference: HFD SOP 900.25

1. Identify Substance (ERG)
Use ERG Orange pages to match placard ID. Consult Green pages for protective distances. Reference: ERG 2024
2. Isolation Zones
Establish Hot/Warm/Cold zones: - Hot: contamination area - Warm: decon corridor - Cold: support/staging Reference: HFD SOP 900.23; 1000.01; 1000.02
3. DECIDE Model
Detect, Estimate risk, Choose action, Identify options, Do response, Evaluate. Guides tactical decision-making. Reference: Company Officer, Hazmat chapter
4. PPE Selection
Choose Level A‚ÄìD based on product and ERG. Level A for unknown/high risk; D for low-risk. Reference: ERG, HFD SOP 400.12
5. Decontamination
Set up wet decon line in warm zone. Use water/soap solutions; protect runoff. Reference: HFD SOP 1000.02
