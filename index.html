🔥 Structure Fire Operations

Size-up & Risk Assessment
  • Establish command & location.
  • Identify structure type, occupancy, exposures.
  • Check water supply & hydrant locations.
  • Review pre-plans & construction details.
NFPA 1710 5.2.2: First due engine—arrival & crew setup within 4 min; ensure 4‑person crew staffing per engine.

References: SOP 900.44 | Essentials 7e Ch 12 | NFPA 1710 5.2.2 | Company Officer 6e Ch 3

Incident Command & ICS
  • Implement NIMS ICS: Command, Ops, Logistics, Planning, Finance.
  • Establish divisions/zones; ensure 4 × 4 accountability tags.
  • PAR every 15 minutes; document on ICS 201/202 forms.
Company Officer 6e Ch 4: Assign clear ICS roles, maintain communication via tactical worksheets.

SOP 900.03 | Essentials 7e Ch 2 | NFPA 1561 4.1.2 | Company Officer 6e Ch 4

Fire Attack & Tactics
  • Select proper hoseline: 1¾" (150–175 gpm) or 2½" (200–250 gpm).
  • Coordinate ventilation before interior attack.
  • Maintain backup line; assign RIT per SOP.
Essentials 7e Ch 14: Advance hoseline with a two‑in/two‑out safety rule; control fire flow rates.

SOP 900.11 | Essentials 7e Ch 14 | NFPA 1710 5.2.4 | Company Officer 6e Ch 6

🚑 Emergency Medical Services

Adult Cardiac Arrest
  • High‑quality CPR: 100–120 cpm, 5–6 cm depth, full chest recoil.
  • Rotate compressors every 2 min; pause pauses < 10 s.
  • AED: shock every 2 min; follow one‑shock protocol.
  • Post‑ROSC: maintain SBP > 90 mmHg, SpO₂ 94–99%.
AHA 2020 Guidelines: Compression fraction > 60%; minimize interruptions; integrate advanced airway management only if CPR quality maintained.

SOP 1300.1 | AHA 2020 Adult CA | Essentials 7e Ch 20 | NFPA 1917 8.4.2

Stroke Assessment & Transport
  • FAST exam; assess facial droop, arm drift, speech.
  • Check glucose; correct hypo‑/hyperglycemia.
  • Pre‑notify stroke center: include last‑known‑well & NIHSS score.
KBEMS Stroke Protocol 2024: Door‑to‑needle goal < 60 min; CT+ stroke center transport if within 20 min.

SOP 1300.3 | KBEMS 2024 Stroke | Essentials 7e Ch 18 | NFPA 1917 8.6.1

Pediatric Care
  • Use Broselow tape; weight‑based drug dosing & equipment sizing.
  • CPR: depth one‑third AP diameter, 100–120 cpm.
  • Defib energy: 2 J/kg escalating to 4 J/kg.
PALS 2023: IO access if IV > 90 s; epinephrine 0.01 mg/kg every 3–5 min.

SOP 1300.9 | PALS 2023 | Essentials 7e Ch 22 | NFPA 1901

Airway Management
  • OPA/NPA adjuncts; two‑person BVM with 100% O₂.
  • ETCO₂: target 35–45 mmHg; confirm every 5 min.
  • RSI meds per local protocol: etomidate, succinylcholine.
NFPA 1917 8.2: Mandatory continuous capnography for all intubations; document waveform.

SOP 1300.8 | Essentials 7e Ch 17 | NFPA 1917 8.2 | NREMT Airway Sheet

      Ladder Placement & Stabilization
      

        
    
          
  • Deploy outriggers on firm ground; level within 5°.
    • 
          
  • Set ladder at 75° (1:4 ratio) for optimal climb angle.
    •

🚨 NIMS, Triage & Mass Casualty Response


  

    NIMS Command Overview
    

      
    
        
  • Establish Incident Command: First arriving officer becomes IC per SOP 900.03.
    • 
        
  • Set up ICS Structure: Command, Operations, Planning, Logistics, Finance/Admin.
    • 
        
  • Start IAP (Incident Action Plan): Even if verbal for first ops period.
    • 
        
  • Maintain Unity of Command: One supervisor per responder, no freelancing.
    • 
      

      

        Reference: NIMS 100/200 | SOP 900.03 | Essentials 7e Ch 2
      

    

  


  

    START Triage for Mass Casualty
    

      
    
        
  • Green (Minor): Walking wounded.
    • 
        
  • Yellow (Delayed): Cannot walk but respirations <30, cap refill <2s, obeys commands.
    • 
        
  • Red (Immediate): Breathing >30/min, cap refill >2s, or unable to follow commands.
    • 
        
  • Black (Deceased/Expectant): Not breathing after airway reposition attempt.
    • 
      

      

        Reference: START Triage System | KBEMS MCI Protocol | Essentials 7e Ch 23
      

    

  


  

    Mass Casualty Incident (MCI) Priorities
    

      
    
        
  • Scene Safety: Hazards, secondary devices, structural collapse.
    • 
        
  • Establish Triage Officer: Assign first competent medical responder.
    • 
        
  • Request Resources: MCI Trailers, ambulances, air evac if needed.
    • 
        
  • Coordinate Transport: Immediate transport for Red patients first.
    • 
      

      

        Reference: NIMS 700/800 | SOP 900.21 | NFPA 1561 | KBEMS MCI Policies
      

    

  


  

    Unified Command for Multi-Agency Response
    

      
    
        
  • Include Fire, EMS, Law Enforcement, EMA in Unified Command post.
    • 
        
  • Share IAPs across disciplines to align priorities and safety plans.
    • 
        
  • Use common radio channels assigned via ICS 205.
    • 
      

      

        Reference: NIMS 300/400 | SOP 900.07 | Company Officer 6e Ch 8

🎯 Mission Statement

  

    The Universal Emergency Response Compendium is designed to strengthen the capabilities, knowledge, and decision-making skills of firefighters, EMTs, Hazmat technicians, and company officers by providing an integrated, accessible, and dynamic resource grounded in HFD SOPs, national standards, and real-world operational experience.


    By unifying critical fireground operations, emergency medical procedures, hazardous materials response, and command-level protocols into a single, mobile-optimized platform, we aim to prepare today’s emergency responders for the extreme demands of modern incident response — protecting lives, property, and each other.
